Sketch 10

Jumpsuit + sweater

Nana Varchenko

Kyiv, Ukraine

In her childhood Nana didn't dream to be a designer, she just wanted to be herself, to wear the clothes she liked and found good for her. For that reason, she customized all the things her parents bought her. She applied her own patterns on the clothes and could make one thing from three. She wanted to become a photoartist, but her parents insisted on marketing education. An interesting fact is that it was her work in marketing that led her to fashion design. On her friends’ recommendation Nana started to work with a young foreign designer as a marketing specialist and understood that fashion design was her vocation. She was thrilled to observe how the collections were created. Some time passed and she decided to try her hand in fashion design and started studying. Now she is going to launch two her own brands and feels that she is right where she belongs.

The designer of this model has chosen the motives of the 70s of the last century. In her view, it is one of the brightest and most experimental periods. She supposes that was the time when people became bolder while choosing and combining clothes. This tendency was a sign of the whole epoch that created new trends. The main emphasis of the 70s fashion was on multilayering and ability to combine.

The ability to combine means that even if you have just a few items in your wardrobe you can create plenty of looks combining them with each other, and each new thing is to match at least three of those you already have.

The unisex style grew in popularity in the 70s too, that allowed men and women to wear the same clothes and look stylish and original. The author of the sketch has chosen a jumpsuit as a base element of her model. A jumpsuit is a multipurpose thing.

If you combine it with classic shirts and accessories you will have an elegant look that can be suitable for office work or business meetings. If you choose a warm sweater or a T-shirt your look will completely change and become more comfortable for spending time in a café with friends or walking in a park.
